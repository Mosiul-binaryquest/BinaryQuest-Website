Bangladesh Labour Act Compliance: What Every HR Manager Needs to Know in 2025

Critical compliance points
  • Minimum wage: BDT 12,500/month (revised Dec 2023)
  • Gratuity: mandatory after 5 years of continuous service
  • Maternity leave: 16 weeks fully paid (post 2013 amendment)
  • Festival bonus: minimum 1 month's basic wage twice yearly

The Bangladesh Labour Act 2006 and its subsequent amendments define a comprehensive set of obligations for every employer. 5 Signs Your Business Has Outgrown Manual Inventory Management — and What to Do About It

The 5 warning signs
  • You discover stockouts only when a customer calls to complain
  • Month-end stock reconciliation takes more than one day
  • Different people quote different stock figures for the same item
  • You over-order to avoid stockouts — and then write off expired stock
  • Your finance team can't trust the inventory value on the balance sheet

BinaryQuest's supply chain consultants have reviewed hundreds of inventory operations across South Asia, and the warning signs that a business has outgrown its current approach are remarkably consistent. The most expensive one — by far — is the "buffer stock" mentality: over-ordering everything because nobody trusts the inventory data, then writing off slow-moving and expired stock at year-end. One BinaryQuest client in the pharmaceutical distribution sector was writing off BDT 8 million annually in expired stock. After implementing Odoo inventory with AI-driven reorder points and expiry date tracking, their write-offs fell to BDT 800,000 in the first year — a 10× return on their ERP investment from a single metric.
